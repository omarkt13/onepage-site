The bet

You've tried grayscale, app timers, Do Not Disturb. Willpower against a supercomputer in your pocket loses every time. Distance is the only intervention that holds — OnePage makes distance workable.

The design filter for every feature: does it pull you back into a scroll loop? Then it's out.

What gets through

Six channels. That's the list.

  • Calls — see who, answer to your earbuds via your phone
  • Messages — WhatsApp, SMS, Signal, Telegram via your phone
  • Calendar — day view, accept & decline direct sync
  • Email — triage: read, archive, flag direct sync
  • Music & podcasts — play, skip, volume via your phone
  • Alarms & reminders on device

Nothing relays unless you allow it. The default is calls and starred contacts only; every additional app is a deliberate opt-in.

What it refuses

The feature list that matters.

  • Browser no rabbit holes
  • Feeds nothing to pull down
  • App store nothing to install
  • Camera nothing to post
  • Color, video, sound ink doesn't scroll
  • Subscription on principle

Each refusal is permanent. A screen that ghosts when you scroll is a screen nobody doomscrolls on — e-ink isn't the compromise here, it's the moat.

OnePage talks to your phone over Bluetooth when it's near, and through the Dock — a charging cradle that parks your phone at home — when it isn't.

On the page

Messaging Center

Every relayed notification lands in one quiet, thread-grouped ledger: what arrived, from whom, when. You check it on your schedule and choose what deserves a response. It turns "I might be missing something" into a list — which is the entire psychological job.

Handle something on the phone and it clears here automatically. Reading here never marks anything read there.

OnePage Messages

A first-party chat channel for the people you actually talk to. Full two-way conversation on both platforms — and your household needs one device, not two: your partner replies from their phone's app, you read it on ink.

Messages route to wherever you really are: docked and unplugged, they arrive on OnePage live; otherwise they fall back to the phone. Never stranded.

Circle

A quiet screen showing which of your people are unplugged right now — "Sara docked her phone 2h ago." Docking your phone starts to feel like joining, not missing out.

A state, never a score. No streaks, no counters, no notifications when someone docks. Mutual, opt-in, per contact, and it doesn't remember yesterday.

The iPhone honesty section

What we can't do on iPhone — and won't pretend to.

Apple lets accessories read notifications and act on calls, but reserves free-text replies for the Apple Watch. Most companion devices hand-wave this. We'd rather you know exactly what you're buying.

On iPhone, OnePage is a triage device, not a conversation device: let 90% of pings die quietly, acknowledge the 10% that matter with a fast, short reply, and flag the rest for later — composing essays on e-ink was never the point. If Apple opens the door (EU pressure is active), replies ship in an update.
